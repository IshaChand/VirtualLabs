Resistance Welding

Theory

This is a thermoelectric method where heat can be generated at the edge planes of welding plates because of electric resistance and a weld joint can be created by applying low-pressure to these plates. The working principle of resistance welding is the generation of heat because of electric resistance. Whenever the current flows through electric resistance, then heat will be generated. The same working principle can be used within the electric coil. The generated heat will depend on material’s resistance, applied current, conditions of a surface, applied the current time period.

This heat generation takes place because of the energy conversion from electric to thermal. The resistance welding formula for heat generation is

H = I²RT

where,
‘H’ is a generated Heat, and the unit of heat is a joule
‘I’ is an electric current, and the unit of this is ampere
‘R’ is an electric resistance, and the unit of this is Ohm
‘T’ is the time of current flow, and the unit of this is second

Types:

  • Spot welding
  • Seam welding
  • Projection welding
  • Upset welding
  • Flash butt welding

Advantages:

  • Less skilled welder can also operate the spot welding machine.
  • Most of the spot welding machines are semi-automatic or automatic.
  • This process is suitable for mass production as it takes less time.
  • No filler material is used during the process.
  • Heating of metal takes place in a very small region which saves the metal from distortion.
  • This method generates weld with no application of any flux, filler material, and shielding gases.
  • Dissimilar metals can also be welded by using a spot welding process.

Disadvantages:

  • Machines used for spot welding are complex and they are expensive.
  • It is suitable for welding the metal plates having thickness of 3mm to 4mm (by using lap joints only).
  • Certain resistance welding processes are limited only to lap joints.
  • Spot welds have low tensile and fatigue strength.
  • Equipment is not portable as it is heavy.
  • The cost of equipment is high.

Applications:

  • Resistance welding is used in mass production for welding sheet metal, wire and tubes.
  • It is used in welding bars, boxes, cans, rods, pipes and frames metals of medium and high resistance materials such as steel, stainless steel, monei metal and silicon bronze which are easy to weld.
  • It is used in welding aircraft and automobile parts.
  • It is used for making cutting tools.
  • It is used for making fuel tanks of cars, tractors etc.
  • It is used for making wire fabrics, grids, grills, mesh weld, containers etc.

How it Works:
Spot welding principle: In spot welding process, a low voltage and a high current (around 15000 ampere) is passed through the joint for a very short time. This high ampere current heats up the joint and melts it. The pressure applied to the joint fuses the metal parts with each other and final welded joint is obtained.

During the spot welding process, the higher current is required to get more heat at the workpiece interface. Because of this reason, the electrodes of spot welding are made up of copper (as copper has less electrical resistance). The pressure is applied either mechanically, hydraulically or pneumatically.
