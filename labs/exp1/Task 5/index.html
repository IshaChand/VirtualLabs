Aim

To join two metals using arc welding process.

Electrode

An electrode is used to conduct current through a work-piece to fuse two pieces together. It is made out of materials with a similar composition to the metal being welded. Electrodes need to be stored in a moisture free environment and carefully removed from any package.

Electrode Holder

An electrode holder is a device that is used to hold the electrode during the welding process. It is a clamping device that holds the electrode secure enough to enable you to weld in a variety of positions. They come in many different sizes and current capacity.

Electrode Cable

The electrode cable and the ground cable are important parts ofthe welding circuit. They must be very flexible and have a tough heat-resistant insulation.Connections at the electrode holder, the ground clamp, and at the power source lugs must besoldered or well crimped to assure low electrical resistance. The cross-sectional area of thecable must be sufficient size to carry the welding current with a minimum of voltage drop.

Sheet metal

Sheet metal is metal formed into thin, flat pieces, usually by an industrial process. Sheet metal is one of the fundamental forms used in metalworking, and it can be cut and bent into a variety of shapes.

Arc Welding Setup

The arc welding equipment includes a power source, welding cables, electrode holder, chipping hammer, wire brush, hand screen, protective clothing, etc. Arc welding power source comes in two types direct current (DC) and alternative current (AC). Welding cables are made of aluminum or copper cables insulated with either red, black, or blue color.

Safety Measures

Gloves

Welding gloves can protect you from electric shock, flames, hot parts, sharp or flying metal, and arc rays. Proper gloves are the first line of defense against the hand hazards of welding and cutting.

Safety Measures

Helmet

Welding helmets are necessary to prevent arc eye, a painful condition where the cornea is inflamed. Welding helmets can also prevent retina burns, which can lead to a loss of vision.
